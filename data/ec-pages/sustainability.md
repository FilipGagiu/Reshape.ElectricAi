Nature Is Our Dance Floor
That’s why, when the party is over, we pay attention to everything we leave behind, so future generations of festival goers will be able not only to rock their bodies in the exact pristine nature but, most importantly, live on a rockin’ planet.
At Electric Castle, we believe that when it comes to any sustainable action it shouldn’t be a one-hit wonder, but an all-timer.
Thus, our environmental efforts are constant and more in tune with nature.
One edition at a time.
And all these wouldn't be possible without a collective effort. Our take on sustainability is community-based. We engage in different initiatives and partnerships with NGOs, associations, and key partners all year long to be able to bring about real change.
We know there is still room for improvement, but while we’ll lead the way, we’ll need a hand here and there.
Scroll down and discover all about our sustainable actions and how you can do your bit.

## GREENERY
Evergrowing Forest
supported by
Over 7.000

Together with our friends at BT, we started to transform EC Village into a place with many shaded areas and green spaces. From a festival campsite to a locals’ park open for all. This year we planted 2500 trees, with a total of 7000 trees planted since 2023. And we’ll keep doing so for the next 2 years. Twice a year, during Spring and Autumn.

## WASTE MANAGEMENT
by
“Clean up after ourselves” pledge
Building the festival from the ground up uses a lot of resources. That’s why we aim to recycle the pre & post festival waste.
Cooking oil turned into candles? Yes, really!
With the help of OilRight, a social enterprise, we collected nearly a ton (965 kg, to be exact) of used cooking oil from our vendors, which is now being turned into eco-friendly candles. It’s the little things that add up to big changes!

Community
Creating sustainable communities isn't just about the now; it's about laying the foundation of a place where future generations can live too. About enhancing our quality of life while being mindful of our environment. Local communities play a vital role in driving sustainable development initiatives, in and outside the festival, as they are intimately connected to the natural resources, social fabric, and economic well-being of their regions.
Imagine our community as a garden, where each seed grows from shared resources and stories, enriching and purifying our shared space, thriving through our collective care for today and tomorrow.
